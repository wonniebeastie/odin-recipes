Build Your Own Stir-Fry Wokshop

Base + Stir Fry + Sauce = Easy, peasy, lemon-squeezy COMPLETE MEAL.

Sauces

Heat & thicken up the sauce separately, mix into veggies + protein AT THE END (can be added to noodles if using noodles). Adding salt earlier in the process draws out too much water from the veggies.
  • Classic Garlic-Ginger Black Bean Sauce
    • 1/4 cup broth
    • 3 Tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
    • 2 Tbsp hoisin sauce
    • 2 Tbsp golden or brown Lakanto
    • 2 Tbsp finely minced garlic
    • 1 Tbsp grated ginger
    • 1 Tbsp rice vinegar
    • 1 Tbsp cornstarch
    • 1 tsp fine onion powder
    • 1 Tbsp sriracha (optional)
    • Dried red peppers (optional)
    • 1 Tbsp toasted sesame oil (after cooking & heat is turned off)

  • Dark Unami Bomb Sauce
    • 2 Tbsp + 1.2 Tbsp dark soy sauce
    • 2 Tbsp oyster sauce
    • 1 Tbsp mirin
    • 2 tsp golden or brown Lakanto
    • 1 tsp rice vinegar
    • 1/2 tsp ~ 1 tsp fine onion powder (optional)
    • 1/4 tsp ~ 1/2 tsp fine garlic powder or several cloves of minced garlic (optional)

  • Thai Peanut Stir Fry Sauce
    • 2 Tbsp creamy peanut butter
    • 2 Tbsp rice vinegar
    • 2 Tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
    • 1 Tbsp golden or brown Lakanto
    • 1 tsp fine garlic powder
    • 1 tsp fine onion powder
    • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
    • cayenne pepper to taste

  • Sweet Chili Sauce
    • Ginger
    • Garlic
    • 4 Tbsp Thai sweet chili sauce
    • 1 Tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
    • 1 Tbsp dark soy sauce
    • 2 tsp fish sauce
    • 2 Tbsp peanut butter (Optional)

  • Sweet & Sour Pineapple Sauce
    • 1/2 cup Lakanto
    • 1/4 cup ketchup
    • 1/4 cup white or rice vinegar
    • 1/4 cup water
    • 1 Tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
    • 1 tsp fine garlic powder
    • 1 tsp fine onion powder
    • 1 bell pepper
    • 1/2 cup chopped pineapple
    • 1/2 cup red onion
    • Sesame seeds and scallions for garnish

  • Tang Su Yuk Sauce (Chinese style Korean Sweet & Sour sauce)
    • 2 Tbsp + 1/2 Tbsp corn starch
    • 2 Tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
    • 1/4 cup Lakanto
    • 1/4 cup white/brown rice/apple vinegar
    • 1 cup water
    • 2 rings of pineapple, chopped
    • 1 small apple, sliced

Base

Cook according to directions on packaging/your rice cooker.
  • Rice (Brown rice is healthier than white, short/medium grain for moist, sticky rice, long grain for drier, fall-apart rice)
  • Wheat or rice noodles (Rinse with cold water after cooking. Drain. Add noodles to HOT wok/pan with sauce)

Meaty Stuff

  • Firm tofu
  • Tempeh
  • Seitan
  • Mushrooms (King oyster, wood ear, etc.)
  • Frozen shelled edamame
  • Chickpeas (Canned)
  • Lentils (Cooked)
  • Soy curls
  • Peas (Frozen)
  • Eggs
  • If using meat, use sparingly, cook half-way before adding veggies

Veggies/Fruit

Crank the heat all the way up. Use an oil with a high smoke point, but not too much! Don't crowd pan.
  • Bags of frozen pre-cut veggies for stir fry
  • Asparagus
  • Baby Corn
  • Bean Sprouts
  • Bell Peppers
  • Bok Choy (Fresh)
  • Broccoli
  • Cabbage (Fresh)
  • Carrots
  • Dried Red Peppers (For spicy)
  • Eggplant
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Green Beans
  • Green Onions/Scallions
  • Pineapple
  • Red Onions (Higher in nutritional value than yellow or white)
  • Spinach (Frozen)
  • Squash
  • Water Chestnuts (Canned or Frozen)
  • Zucchini

    • Note: Haven't tried it yet, but have heard that a cast iron pan/wok can help to go from zero to hundred real fast and retain enough heat to make veggies cook fast & prevent soggy-ness; A quick rinse or blanching of frozen veggies supposedly also helps.
